Hull Classes & Ship Systems

Your ship isn't just a vehicle — it's your home, your weapon, and the only thing standing between your crew and the void. Choose your hull. Know its limits.

Scout-Class
Fast · Fragile · Independent
Hull Points
40
Shield Points
30
Base Power
4
Weapon Slots
2

Built for speed and stealth over survivability. Scout-class vessels are the preferred hull for solo operators, advance recon units, and crews who survive by never being where the enemy expects. Limited weapon hardpoints means engagements must be decisive — you won't win a slugfest in one of these. What you will do is run circles around anything heavier.

Frigate-Class
Balanced · Versatile · Standard Crew
Hull Points
80
Shield Points
50
Base Power
6
Weapon Slots
4

The default starting hull for most crews. The Frigate-class is the backbone of independent spaceflight — enough room for a full four-person crew, enough weapons to handle a fight, and enough speed to flee one. No faction controls frigate production; they're built everywhere, serviced everywhere, and parts are available at every port above Sec-2.

Cruiser-Class
Heavy · Powerful · Slow Turns
Hull Points
150
Shield Points
90
Base Power
9
Weapon Slots
7

Cruisers are warships dressed as cargo haulers, or cargo haulers that got into too many fights and kept bolting on guns. They hit hard, take punishment, and have the cargo space to make serious trade runs — at the cost of maneuverability. A skilled Pilot is non-negotiable in a Cruiser; without one, you'll spend half of combat turning to face your enemy.

⚓
Dreadnought-Class
Massive · Near-Indestructible · Crew of 8+
Hull Points
300
Shield Points
180
Base Power
14
Weapon Slots
12

Dreadnoughts are not acquired — they are seized, inherited, or built by people with too much money and a grudge. Operating one requires a minimum crew of eight to fully staff all stations. In the hands of a coordinated team, a Dreadnought can hold a sector. In the hands of an under-crewed party, it's a very expensive flying coffin with excellent interior lighting.

Common Ship Upgrades
Available at Sec-3+ ports · Prices vary by faction
🛡️ Ablative Plating
+20 Hull Points. Increases ship mass, reducing top speed by 10%. Stackable twice.
⚡ Overclocked Reactor
+2 Power per round. Increases base Heat generation by 1 per round. Requires a Tier 2 reactor slot.
📡 ECM Suite
Captain's Scramble Signals ability affects an additional target. Requires Comms station upgrade.
🔭 Deep Scan Array
Reveals all ship contacts in a full sector radius. Passive; active scan costs 1 Power.
🚀 Warp Stabilizers
Reduces FLY DC for emergency warp jumps by 3. Prevents warp drift in Sec-0 zones.
🧪 Med-Bay Module
Crew members can recover 1d6 HP per long rest while aboard. Requires a dedicated ship room.

Reactor Tiers
Power output determines action economy
Tier 1
4–6 PC
Tier 2
8–10 PC
Tier 3
12–16 PC

Reactor tier determines how many Power Points are available each round. Higher tier reactors also increase Heat threshold before systems start failing. Reactor upgrades are the most expensive single purchase in the game and are typically found only at major drydocks.
