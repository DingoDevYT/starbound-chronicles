Locations & Sectors

The known galaxy is divided into Security Sectors — from the iron-law Core Worlds of Sec-5 to the lawless void of Sec-0. Everything in between is up for grabs.

The Sector Grid

The universe doesn't rely on arbitrary cosmic territory names. Navigation, cartography, and biological origin points are mapped onto a digital coordinate grid that functions like a chessboard across the observable universe — Planet Name, Star System, Galaxy, SECTOR COORDINATE (e.g. Earth, Sol System, Milky Way, SECTOR E4). The grid runs from Sector A0 at one diagonal extreme to Sector J9 at the furthest opposite boundary — the edge of what's currently navigable before the laws of physics get "wonky" (the Farlands).

Mineral/Solid Core (A–C) Temperate Cluster (C–G) Liquid Fluidity (F–G) Gaseous Expanse (H–J)

Life clusters predominantly within the temperate central bands. The closer a species is to an extreme edge of the chessboard, the more their physical makeup reflects that environment.

Mineral Structure Zone

Volatile pressures and harsh gravity force life into dense, high-mass matrices — mineral accretion instead of soft cell division, growth dictated by a core stone embedded in the cranium.

Kragol — Mintonas, SECTOR B2

Temperate Flesh Zone

The structural middle ground. Balanced molecular consistency gives rise to carbon, calcium, and water-based species with complex muscle systems and endoskeletons.

Humans — Earth, SECTOR E4

Liquid Fluidity Zone

Low-viscosity environments where rigid skeletons are a disadvantage. Life is gelatinous and amorphous, highly malleable, lacking standard skeletal systems.

Myxos — Juniper, SECTOR G5

Gaseous Expanse

Physical mass breaks down entirely near the fringe. Life here is airborne vapour, cloud-based anomaly, or sentient gas colony — barely considered "living" by most standards.

Unmapped — approaching SECTOR J9
Factions & Groups

Four powers shape most of galactic politics — a peacekeeping council, a mining conglomerate, a code-bound nomad network, and the pirates that answer to no one.

GAC Galactic Allegiance Council

A united front formed by a large majority of intelligent space-travelling species, working toward peace and prosperity across all sectors. Open to any species willing to cooperate on their terms. Responsible for most "military" efforts in the galaxy, defending worlds unable to defend themselves from pirates and smugglers.

Founding worlds: Humans (Earth, Sol System, Milky Way — SECTOR E4) · Myxos (Juniper, Myx System, Nebulous Way — SECTOR G5) · Arborans (Arkanos, Calamitus Field — SECTOR F3)
KEC Kragol Extraction Conglomerate

A corporate superpower focused entirely on harvesting natural resources to fuel unchecked industrialisation and expansion. The Kragol serve as sole corporate executives — acquisition of mineral-rich territory is existential, since their lithivore diet is entirely rock, crystal, and unrefined metal. The KEC systematically uses enslaved Skitterlings for manual labour, exploiting their hyper-energy nature as miners who need zero food management — they simply eat the subterranean scrap while they work.

Founding worlds: Kragol (Mintonas, Krag System, The Great Ring — SECTOR B2) · Skitterlings (Captured Labour Colonies, Various Sectors)
SNA Scavenger Nomad Allegiance

A widespread network of planet-hopping spacefarers bound not by government but by a strict, unyielding code of honour. The galaxy's "Robin Hoods" — stealing from wealthy corporate entities to redistribute credits and supplies to impoverished frontier worlds. Scavengers never attack one another and unite instantly against major threats like the GAC, corporate strike teams, or pirates. Highly diverse crews, attracting desperate species who refuse to morally compromise into outright crime.

Founding worlds: Humans (The Moon Colonies, Jupiter System, Milky Way — SECTOR E4) · Defected Kragol (Mintonas Outskirts, Krag System — SECTOR B2) · Skitterlings (Escaped Labour Colonies, Unknown Sectors)
VP Void Pirates

Not a unified organisation, but a violent classification given to independent outlaw fleets rejecting all galactic law in pursuit of raw wealth. Standing in direct moral opposition to the Scavengers, VP crews ambush civilian vessels, terrorise defenceless colonies, and accumulate credits through brutal fleet violence. Lacking central leadership, VP packs are notoriously fractured — captains harbour deep personal grudges, and separate crews will attack each other over salvage just as readily as they'd strike a GAC trading convoy.

Notable crews: Kaelen Outcasts (The Obsidian Fringe, Outer Rim — SECTOR B1) · Human Renegades (The Rust Belts, Sol System — SECTOR E4) · Ex-KEC Mercenaries (The Borderlands, Unmapped Space — SECTOR B2)
Sec-5 — Core Worlds
High security · Federation law enforced · Contraband illegal · Safest travel
HIGH-SEC

The heart of the Galactic Federation. Sec-5 worlds operate under full Patrol Fleet coverage, biometric docking registries, and zero tolerance for unlicensed weapons above civilian grade. Prices are highest here, opportunities are fewest, and if you're crew with a record, every port checkpoint is a liability. That said — Federation citizen protections are real. Contracts hold. Murder has consequences. For most spacers, Sec-5 is where you go to spend your winnings, not where you make them.

🏛️ Aethon Prime
Federation capital. Home of the Grand Assembly and the Patrol Fleet Academy. Heavily surveilled. Tourist traffic is enormous; smuggling traffic is suicidal.
🌿 Verdannis Station
Arboran-majority agricultural hub. Provides 40% of Core World food supply. Heavily tariffed. The Arboran Growers Collective has more political pull than most navies.
⚕️ Caduceus Orbital
Galaxy's premier medical research station. Myxos-operated. Controlled access — not open to walk-ins, but will treat anyone for the right price (astronomical).
🔬 The Atheneum
Archive station. Petabytes of recorded galactic history, deep-scan data, and pre-Federation records. Free public access. Certain archives are restricted by Federation decree.
Sec-4 — Outer Systems
Patrol presence · Corporate law zones · Moderate risk
HIGH-SEC

Federation law still applies here, but enforcement is patchier. Corporate Security Forces fill the gaps — which means their law applies, not the Federation's, in many zones. Sec-4 is where the real economy happens: mining consortiums, mid-tier trade routes, and the kind of side jobs that aren't technically illegal but absolutely won't be put in writing.

⛏️ The Kragol Belt
Dense asteroid mining corridor. Kragol Clan-run. Three major families control extraction rights; outsiders can contract work or buy freight. Don't short a Kragol on weighing rights.
🏙️ Port Solace
Human-built trade nexus. Has all major faction embassies. Every major corporation has an office here. If you need a legal contract notarized, Port Solace is your stop.
📡 Relay-19
Communication relay station and waypoint stop. Owned by the Comms Authority. Cheap docking, fast resupply, no questions about cargo — unless it beeps on the scanners.
🕸️ Skitter Market
Skitterling-operated bazaar station. Labyrinthine. If it exists, someone is selling it here. Caveat: if someone is selling it, someone else is stealing it.
Sec-3 — Border Space
Mixed jurisdiction · Freelancer friendly · Moderate contraband tolerance
MID-SEC

The sweet spot for independent operators. Federation patrols exist but are infrequent. Local law is set by whoever bought the station. Most freelance contracts originate from Sec-3 posting boards. Also where you'll start hearing stories about Sec-1 and Sec-0 from people who survived them.

⚙️ Irongate Station
Former military drydock, now a civilian repair hub. The best ship upgrades outside Sec-5. Run by a ex-Patrol Fleet admiral who asks no questions and charges accordingly.
🍺 The Coronal Tap
Notorious spacer cantina and information exchange. Everyone passes through eventually. The barkeeper knows twelve ways to say "I didn't hear that."
🌌 Deepwatch Outpost
Independent science station monitoring deep space anomalies. Pays good credits for sensor data. Has a standing wanted list for "items of research interest."
📦 Waystation Delta
Generic-name cargo depot. Currently under three separate claims of ownership. Nobody has resolved this legally. Meanwhile, the cargo keeps flowing.
Sec-2 — Frontier Space
No Federation patrol · Local enforcement only · Dangerous
LOW-SEC

Frontier space is where the galaxy thins out. Supply chains stretch. Prices swing wildly. The local law is whoever's armed. Colonists, hermit operations, resource extraction camps, and cult compounds are all you'll find out here — and they all have reasons they moved to the edge.

🏚️ Dustfall Colony
Human frontier settlement. 3,000 people trying to make a planet livable. Desperate for medical supplies, spare parts, and protection they can't afford.
🦠 The Rot Fields
Myxos biome world, partially terraformed and then abandoned mid-process. The resulting ecosystems are genuinely unknown. Researchers go in. They don't always come back.
⚠️ Freehold Pax
Explicitly anarchist station. No rules by design. Surprisingly stable. Run by consensus committee of sixty people who argue constantly and somehow agree on emergencies.
🔒 The Vault
Rumored high-security storage facility. Nobody knows who owns it. Ships have gone looking. Some come back. The ones that don't are filed as "navigation loss."
Sec-1 — Contested Space
Active conflict · No safe ports · Travel at own risk
LOW-SEC

Contested space means what it says. At least two armed factions are actively fighting over this territory. Supply routes are targets. Stations change hands. The only law is firepower, and the only economy is war profiteering. The rewards are astronomical. So is the body count.

💀 The Graveyard
Site of the Battle of Kerrith Crossing. Six wrecked capital ships drift in mutual orbit. Salvagers work it constantly. Pirates hunt the salvagers. It's a whole ecosystem.
🔴 Redline Station
Nominally neutral resupply point. Both factions use it. Neither attacks it — yet. The moment that calculus changes, everyone on board is collateral.
⚡ The Bleedzone
A 40-lightyear corridor where the Syndicate and the Federation have been skirmishing for eleven years. Nothing built here lasts long enough to get a proper name.
🧨 Boneyard Cache
Hidden weapons depot, origin unknown. Coordinates are worth a fortune. Three different crews claim to have the real coordinates. Two of them are lying. One of them is wrong.
Sec-0 — Null Space
Beyond mapped galaxy · No rescue · No record · No law
NULL-SEC

Null Space begins where the maps end. No faction claims it. No patrol reaches it. Navigation hazards are uncharted, warp drift is extreme without Warp Stabilizers, and anything that lives out here has had no reason to be friendly to outsiders. The only people who go to Null Space on purpose are the ones who can't go anywhere else — and the ones looking for whatever those people are hiding.

👁️ The Aperture
Stable wormhole of unknown origin. Leads somewhere. Nobody who's come back has been able to describe it coherently. Three ships haven't come back at all.
🌑 The Silence
A 12-lightyear region where all communication signals die. Ships enter. Ships exit. What happens inside is reported differently by every crew. The reports share one detail: nothing follows you out.
🏴‍☠️ Exodus Point
Rumored permanent settlement of exiles, war criminals, and anyone for whom Sec-1 wasn't remote enough. If it exists, it's self-sufficient and very unwelcoming.
⭐ The Old Signal
A repeating transmission from deep Null Space, origin estimated at 800 years old. No faction claims to have sent it. Several factions are racing to find the source.
Deep Space Anomalies
Unexplained phenomena · Research opportunities · Extreme risk
ANOMALOUS

Not every location is on a map or under a flag. The galaxy produces phenomena that defy current physics and reward the brave — or punish the foolish.

🌀 Tide Rift
Gravitational shear zone. Ships that pass through at the wrong angle get torn apart. Ships that pass through at the right angle arrive somewhere else entirely. Nobody's mapped the exits.
🌈 The Prism
Radiation cloud that interferes with all electronic systems — but inside the dead zone, ancient tech from before the Federation somehow still functions.
🕳️ The Drain
A gravity sink with no central mass. Pulls ships in. Three have escaped so far, all with stories that contradict each other. All three crews retired immediately after.
🔮 Null-Echo Site
A derelict structure of non-human construction, origin unknown, age unknown. Completely intact. Every sensor pass shows it empty. Everyone who's docked reports not being alone.
