Core Rulebook

Infinite Stars: Core Rulebook

Alpha v1.0 — The foundational mechanical framework for players and Directors alike. Fast-paced action, cooperative crew dynamics, and an unrestrictive open-world structure.

Alpha v1.0 d20 System Cooperative Ship Combat Open Sandbox
Section 01

Game Overview & Philosophy

Infinite Stars is designed to prioritize fast-paced action, collaborative crew dynamics, and an unrestrictive, open-world structure. Unlike traditional campaign-driven RPGs, Infinite Stars utilizes a modular design built to interact seamlessly with an evolving digital sector map. The core philosophy centers on a shared asset: your crew's starship.

Director's Note

The Director (DM equivalent) sets the scene, plays the factions, and controls the living galaxy. Players are the crew — each commanding their station, pooling resources, and making decisions that ripple across the open map. No railroading. No predetermined story. Just the void and whatever trouble you fly into.

Section 02

The Core Engine — The Four "F"s

All actions in Infinite Stars — whether traversing a lawless space station on foot or exchanging laser fire with a pirate cruiser — are resolved using a d20 system governed by four primary statistics. Ditch traditional metrics of physical or mental attributes; here, your capabilities are defined by operational functions.

FLY
Manual & Movement
Governs manual dexterity, piloting maneuvers, evasion, reflexes, and physical stealth.
FIX
Technical & Scientific
Governs mechanical engineering, software hacking, weapon modifications, and scientific scanning.
FIGHT
Combat & Force
Governs physical force, personal firearms proficiency, and targeting heavy ship-mounted turrets.
FACE
Social & Electronic
Governs social manipulation, deception, intimidation, trading, and electronic communication warfare.
Section 02 — Resolution

Resolution Mechanics

When a character attempts an action with a risk of failure, they roll a 20-sided die (d20) and add the relevant stat modifier. If the total meets or exceeds the Difficulty Class (DC) set by the Director, the action succeeds. Modifiers are calculated as (stat − 10) ÷ 2, rounded down.

DC Classification Example Actions
10 Easy Bypassing a standard civilian lock · Landing a ship in clear weather · Bribing a low-tier guard
15 Moderate Slicing into a secure corporate network · Executing a combat barrel roll · Negotiating with a rival captain
20 Hard Disabling a military-grade tracking array mid-combat · Haggling with a corrupt sector warlord · Hot-landing on a moving platform
Advantage & Disadvantage

When circumstances strongly favor success, the Director may grant Advantage — roll 2d20 and take the higher result. When circumstances work against the character, they roll at Disadvantage — roll 2d20 and take the lower result. Advantage and Disadvantage cancel each other out regardless of how many sources are stacked.

Section 03

Classes & Crew Roles

Your class defines your specialization on foot and dictates your primary station when operating the crew's collective starship. Each class focuses on one of the Four Fs and unlocks a Core Feature — a unique ability that changes how your ship station operates.

Class Stat Focus Ship Station Core Feature
🚀 The Pilot FLY The Helm Evasive Maneuvers: Expend ship power to instantly dodge incoming missiles.
🔫 The Gunsmith FIGHT Weapons Array Overcharge: Increases weapon damage at the cost of generating high system heat.
⚙️ The Technician FIX Engineering Delta System Reboot: Rapidly cycles energy to bring depleted shields back online.
🗣️ The Smuggler FACE Comms & Sensors Signal Jam: Disrupts enemy targeting matrices, lowering their attack accuracy.
Multiclassing Note

At Director discretion, crew members may train secondary station roles over the course of a campaign. A Gunsmith who spends downtime at the helm under a Pilot's instruction may unlock limited access to Helm maneuvers — at a reduced power cost efficiency.

Section 04

Cooperative Ship Combat

Ship combat is the mechanical heart of Infinite Stars. Instead of controlling independent vessels, the entire party operates a singular ship asset, pooling resources and taking tactical turns simultaneously. Every crew member has a role — and the ship dies if anyone neglects theirs.

Ship Resources

🛡️
SP
Shield Points
First line of defense. Regenerates dynamically when power is routed to shields.
🚢
HP
Hull Points
Physical structural integrity. At zero, the ship is disabled or destroyed. Requires drydock to repair.
⚡
PC
Power Core
The primary engine pool. Generates a static Power Point pool each round based on reactor tier.

The Round Loop

1
Power Generation Phase
The ship reactor refreshes its maximum Power Points for the round. A Tier 1 reactor generates 6 Power, scaling with upgrade level.
2
Crew Consultation Phase
Players discuss and distribute Power Points among stations. A crew member cannot act at their station without assigned Power Points.
3
Action Phase
All station actions execute simultaneously. The Pilot moves the ship; the Smuggler scans or jams; the Gunsmith fires; the Technician mitigates incoming damage.
4
Enemy Response Phase
Enemy vessels execute their designated tactical actions. The Director resolves all hostile moves and applies damage to the crew's ship.
Heat & Overload

Certain actions — particularly Overcharge and emergency power redistribution — generate System Heat. If Heat exceeds the ship's threshold, the overloaded system goes offline until the Technician performs an Engineering check to cool it down. Managing heat is the Technician's primary off-combat responsibility.

Section 05

Open-World Living Map & Factions

Infinite Stars relies on a decentralized sandboxed map. Sectors are assigned Security Ratings and Faction Influence Maps, altering encounter rates and economic values dynamically. Where you fly determines what you risk — and what you stand to gain.

Security Level Classification Economic Modifier Risk Assessment
Sec-5 to Sec-4 High-Sec
Imperial / Military Core		 Standard legal market rates; heavy contraband fine multiplier. Minimal piracy. High local police intervention if laws are breached.
Sec-3 to Sec-1 Low-Sec
The Borderlands / Fringe		 Increased value for rare minerals and black-market items. Moderate pirate activity; law enforcement arrival delayed by 1d4 rounds.
Sec-0 Null-Sec
The Wild Expanse		 Maximum credit payouts; lawless trading parameters. Total anarchy. Extreme hazard zones. Unknown alien threats.
Faction Influence

Beyond security ratings, sectors are contested by major factions — the Imperial Compact, the Free Traders' Union, the Syndicate, and the Void Collective. Faction influence affects prices, quest availability, and which ports will grant you docking rights. Completing faction jobs shifts the influence map, and the results persist across all sessions in the living world.
