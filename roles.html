Crew Roles & Ship Stations

Four roles. One ship. Every crew member controls a station — your class determines your battlefield, your abilities determine how you control it.

4 Core Roles Ship Stations Cooperative Combat Alpha v1.0
⚠️ This section is in active development. Ability costs, unlock trees, and advanced station actions will be detailed in a future update.
🚀
The Pilot
Station: The Helm · Stat Focus: FLY
FLY Primary

The Pilot is the nerve center of any ship operation. Where the Gunsmith blows things up and the Technician keeps them together, the Pilot decides whether the ship lives to fight another round. They are the last line of evasion — and the first one responsible when a warp jump goes sideways. A good Pilot doesn't just fly the ship. They become the ship.

🌀 Evasive Maneuvers
Expend 2 Power to instantly redirect one incoming missile or cannon round. Requires a FLY check DC 12. On failure, the ship still takes half damage.
⚡ Tactical Burn
Spend 3 Power to execute a high-thrust repositioning burn. Moves the ship up to two zones in any direction, ignoring standard movement costs this round.
🔭 Sensor Sweep
Passive. The Pilot may spend 1 Power to reveal hidden threats in the current zone. FLY + Fix check — on success, ambushes within range are preempted for 1 round.
⭐ CORE: Dead Stick Landing
Once per session, the Pilot may attempt a controlled emergency landing on any terrain with zero fuel remaining. FLY DC 18. Success: crew survives intact. Failure: crew survives, ship takes 2d10 Hull damage.
🔫
The Gunsmith
Station: Weapons Array · Stat Focus: FIGHT
FIGHT Primary

The Gunsmith operates the ship's offensive systems — railguns, missile pods, point-defense cannons, and whatever black-market weaponry the Smuggler managed to bolt on at the last port. They are raw, controlled destruction. The art isn't pointing and shooting. It's knowing which weapon to fire, in what sequence, at what heat level, without melting the ship's own reactor.

🔥 Overcharge Shot
Spend 3 Power. Deal +2d6 bonus damage on the next weapons salvo. Generates 2 Heat. If current Heat exceeds threshold, the weapons array goes offline until next round.
🎯 Target Lock
Spend 1 Power to establish a target lock. Next round, all weapons fire against this target have Advantage on the FIGHT roll. Lock breaks if target changes zone.
💥 Scatter Volley
Spend 4 Power. Fire simultaneously at up to 3 targets in the current zone. Each takes base weapon damage. No bonus damage. No targeting lock applies.
⭐ CORE: Controlled Demolition
Once per combat, the Gunsmith may declare a Precision Strike — targeting a specific system on an enemy ship (engines, weapons, shields, comms). On hit, that system is disabled for 1d4 rounds instead of dealing Hull damage.
⚙️
The Technician
Station: Engineering Delta · Stat Focus: FIX
FIX Primary

The Technician is the reason the ship doesn't explode. While everyone else is shooting, evading, or talking their way out of problems, the Technician is duct-taping the reactor casing back together with whatever's available. They manage heat, reroute power, patch hull breaches mid-combat, and are the only crew member capable of performing an emergency system reboot without killing everyone aboard.

🔄 Emergency Reboot
Spend 2 Power. Bring one offline system (weapons, shields, engines) back online immediately. FIX DC 14. On failure, system stays offline but Heat is reduced by 1.
🛡️ Shield Reroute
Spend 3 Power. Transfer Shield Points from one system to another, or regenerate 1d6+FIX Shield Points by draining power from non-critical systems.
🌡️ Heat Vent
Spend 1 Power. Reduce ship Heat by 2. Can be used at any point in the round, even reactively after Overcharge damage is declared.
⭐ CORE: Jury-Rig
Once per session, the Technician can improvise a repair using whatever materials are available. Restore up to 2d10 Hull Points. Requires 1 round of uninterrupted work and a successful FIX DC 16. The repair holds for 24 hours then degrades.
🗣️
The Smuggler
Station: Comms & Sensors · Stat Focus: FACE
FACE Primary

The Smuggler is the information warfare specialist, the deal-maker, the distraction, and the reason the crew didn't have to shoot their way out of that checkpoint. They operate the ship's communication arrays and electronic countermeasures — which means they can jam targeting computers, spoof transponder signals, negotiate docking rights under false credentials, and triangulate enemy positions from passive sensor data before the Gunsmith even has a lock.

📡 Signal Jam
Spend 2 Power. Disrupt enemy targeting matrices for 1 round — all attacks against the crew's ship have Disadvantage. FACE DC 13 to maintain past the first round.
🪪 Transponder Spoof
Spend 1 Power + 1 minute of work. Change the ship's apparent registry and ID in sector databases. Lasts until the end of the current system visit or until actively scanned by military-grade equipment.
🔍 Passive Sweep
Spend 2 Power. Scan the current zone for hidden ships, contraband signals, or distress beacons. FACE + Fix check. Reveals all hidden contacts on success.
⭐ CORE: Just Trust Me
Once per session, the Smuggler can attempt to talk any single non-hostile NPC or faction into doing something they would normally refuse. FACE DC 18. On failure, the NPC becomes hostile but the Smuggler knows exactly what leverage would work next time.
