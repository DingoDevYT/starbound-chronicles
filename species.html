Crew Profiles

Species & Survival Biologies

Five species. Five completely incompatible grocery lists. Whether you're crunching starship hull plating for breakfast or sunbathing under a UV lamp during your off-shift, how your species eats shapes how your crew survives.

5 Playable Species Dietary Mechanics Sandbox Compatible Open Universe
Species 01
Humans
The Adaptable Baselines
Omnivore

The Look

You know 'em, you love 'em. Soft, squishy, and wearing too many layers of space gear.

Dietary Metric

Omnivore. Plant, meat, synthesised — Humans can eat almost anything put in front of them.

⚡ The Galactic Vibe

Intellectual, reasoning, diplomatic. Widely considered the "weirdly resilient cockroaches" of the observable universe — highly tenacious and capable of adapting to almost any role on a starship crew. However, they're notorious for their psychological fragility regarding rations, continuously complaining about the texture and flavour of standard-issue synthetic nutrient paste.

Species 02
The Kragol
The Rock Colossus
Lithivore

The Look

Massive, towering humanoids made of dense, layered shale, obsidian, or granite. They lack traditional mouths, possessing instead a grinding stone maw.

Dietary Metric

Lithivore. Rock, mineral, gemstone, metal ore — cannot digest organic plant matter at all.

⚡ The Galactic Vibe

Industrial, stubborn, formidable, deeply territorial. Their unique relationship with minerals allows them to literally smell quality metals, making them unparalleled Gunsmiths and Technicians. However, they pose a constant structural hazard to their own vessel — if a starship takes severe hull damage, an unsupervised Kragol may instinctively try to snack on the broken bulkheads before repairs can be finalised.

Species 03
The Skitterlings
The Gremlin Rascals
Hyper-Omnivore

The Look

Small, multi-limbed, furry gremlins with oversized ears, massive eyes, and rows of needle-sharp teeth. Think Stitch mixed with a deeply caffeinated ferret.

Dietary Metric

Hyper-Omnivore. Plastic, mechanical, …everything. Nothing kills them — plastic wiring, toxic copper fuel, dead batteries, starship insulation, all fair game.

⚡ The Galactic Vibe

Chaotic, hyper-active, spiteful to Kragol, loyal to Scavengers. Their twitchy reflexes and high-speed cognitive processing make them brilliant, unmatched starship Pilots. The primary hazard of hosting a Skitterling is profound boredom — if a long warp jump leaves them with nothing to do, they are highly prone to eating the ship's navigation computer just to pass the time.

Species 04
The Myxos
The Slime People
Dissolvent

The Look

Translucent, gelatinous humanoids whose internal organs are just floating, glowing chemical cores. They can shift their shape slightly to fit into tight spaces.

Dietary Metric

Dissolvent. Chemicals, waste, organic & inorganic — absorbed directly into their gelatinous bodies and broken down with internal bio-acid.

⚡ The Galactic Vibe

Peaceful, highly malleable, unsettling, cooperative. Unsettling to look at, but indispensable for hazardous maintenance and system Engineering. They serve as excellent sanitation officers on long-haul voyages because they can clean up lethal toxic chemical spills in the reactor corridors by simply slurping up the hazardous material as a savoury evening snack.

Species 05
The Arborans
The Plant Folk
Photosynthetic

The Look

Elegant, bark-skinned humanoids with leaves or exotic alien flora instead of hair. Their skin patterns shift colours depending on the wavelength of the nearest star.

Dietary Metric

Photosynthetic. Direct sunlight, UV lamps, water — they do not eat physical food at all.

⚡ The Galactic Vibe

Calm, composed, observant, fundamentally peaceful. They save a crew an absolute fortune on long-distance grocery bills and ration storage. However, they present a minor domestic hazard, as they are notorious for hogging the starship's primary engineering arrays and reactor vents to "sunbathe" during critical shift changes.

GM Note — Dietary Sandbox Scenarios

Since this is an open sandbox where players can choose any entry point, these dietary restrictions create natural tension and opportunity. Landing on a lawless Sec-0 station, for example, might mean standard Human food is heavily marked up — but raw metal scrap for the Kragol is dirt cheap, and the Arborans just need to find a decent UV source. Every resupply run becomes a multi-species negotiation.
