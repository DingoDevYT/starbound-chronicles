Crew Profiles

Species & Survival Biologies

Six species. Six completely incompatible grocery lists. Whether you're crunching starship hull plating for breakfast or sunbathing under a UV lamp during your off-shift, how your species eats shapes how your crew survives.

6 Playable Species Dietary Mechanics Sandbox Compatible Open Universe
🧑
Species 01
Humans
The Adaptable Baselines
Omnivore

The Look

You know 'em, you love 'em. Soft, squishy, and wearing too many layers of space gear.

Dietary Metric

Omnivore. Can only consume organic plants, meats, and processed space rations.

⚡ The Galactic Vibe

Considered the "weirdly resilient cockroaches" of the galaxy. They can eat almost anything standard, but they complain constantly about the taste of synthetic nutrient paste.

Quick Stats
Diet Omnivore
Resupply Easy
Galactic Rep Tenacious
Morale Risk Complains A Lot
Suggested Roles
🎯 Any Role
🗣️ Face
⚙️ Generalist

Species 02
The Kragol
The Rock Colossus
Lithivore

The Look

Massive, towering humanoids made of dense, layered shale, obsidian, or granite. They lack traditional mouths, possessing instead a grinding stone maw.

Dietary Metric

Lithivore. Cannot digest organic plant matter at all. Instead, they eat rocks, raw minerals, gemstone deposits, and unrefined metal ore.

⚡ The Galactic Vibe

They make incredible Gunsmiths or Technicians because they can literally smell quality metal. If the ship takes hull damage, a Kragol might try to snack on the broken bulkhead before the Technician can fix it.

Quick Stats
Diet Lithivore
Resupply Dirt Cheap
Galactic Rep Formidable
Hazard Eats the Ship

Species 03
The Skitterlings
The Gremlin Rascals
Hyper-Omnivore

The Look

Small, multi-limbed, furry gremlins with oversized ears, massive eyes, and rows of needle-sharp teeth. Think Stitch mixed with a deeply caffeinated ferret.

Dietary Metric

Hyper-Omnivore. Can safely ingest absolutely anything. Plastic wiring, toxic copper fuel, dead batteries, starship insulation — nothing kills them.

⚡ The Galactic Vibe

Absolute chaos elements. They are brilliant, twitchy Pilots, but the crew has to constantly monitor them to ensure they don't eat the ship's navigation computer when they get bored during long warp jumps.

Quick Stats
Diet Hyper-Omnivore
Resupply None Needed
Galactic Rep Chaotic
Hazard Eats the Nav Computer
Suggested Roles
🚀 Pilot
🔍 Scout
💣 Demolitions

Species 04
The Myxos
The Slime People
Dissolvent

The Look

Translucent, gelatinous humanoids whose internal organs are just floating, glowing chemical cores. They can shift their shape slightly to fit into tight spaces.

Dietary Metric

Dissolvent. Like the Skitterlings, they can eat anything — but they do it by absorbing objects directly into their gelatinous bodies and dissolving them with internal bio-acid.

⚡ The Galactic Vibe

Excellent for maintenance and Engineering. They are fantastic at cleaning up toxic chemical spills on the ship because they literally just slurp up the hazardous waste as a savoury evening snack.

Quick Stats
Diet Dissolvent
Resupply Eats Waste
Galactic Rep Unsettling
Hazard Low (Usually)

Species 05
The Arborans
The Plant Folk
Photosynthetic

The Look

Elegant, bark-skinned humanoids with leaves or exotic alien flora instead of hair. Their skin patterns shift colours depending on the wavelength of the nearest star.

Dietary Metric

Photosynthetic. They do not eat physical food at all. They only require a steady supply of fresh water and regular exposure to light — either natural sunlight or specialised high-output UV lamps on the ship.

⚡ The Galactic Vibe

Calm, observant, and deeply practical. They save the crew a fortune on grocery bills, but they tend to hog the ship's primary engineering lights to "sunbathe" during their off-work hours.

Species 06
The Kaelen
The Tough Alien Baseline
Strict Herbivore

The Look

Tall, sleek, and elegant like an elf, but with a major alien twist — they have a hardened, mineral-infused skeletal structure and sharp, prehensile horn-crests curving back from their temples that change colour based on atmospheric pressure.

Dietary Metric

Strict Herbivore. Can only process raw plant matter, fruits, and star-grains. Meat or synthetic chemicals make them violently ill.

⚡ The Galactic Vibe

Proud, highly disciplined, and physically tough as old boots due to their reinforced bone structure. They are perfect for the Face or Pilot roles, though they tend to judge the rest of the crew for eating messy, unrefined meats.

GM Note — Dietary Sandbox Scenarios

Since this is an open sandbox where players can choose any entry point, these dietary restrictions create natural tension and opportunity. Landing on a lawless Sec-0 station, for example, might mean standard Human food is heavily marked up — but raw metal scrap for the Kragol is dirt cheap, and the Arborans just need to find a decent UV source. Every resupply run becomes a multi-species negotiation.
